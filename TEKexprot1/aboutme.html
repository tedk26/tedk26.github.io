Ted E. Kissel

Graphic design has always been a passion of mine. I have always loved working

with color. I have a bachelor’s degree in Arts and Sciences from Indiana State

University and I majored in painting. Color has always been an integral part of my paint-

ings, and I enjoy using a diverse variety of color in my design work. When I was nine

years old, I fell in love with the “Terrors Of The Deep” Sea World exhibit logo design. I

have always thought the man who designed it was a genius because through the use of

color, he inspired so much feeling.


When I hear an idea for a new company or product, my mind immediately starts

generating pictures of logos that inspire interest in that company or product. This ability to

think in pictures and the enjoyment of recreating those pictures through art is the

foundation of my love for art. Art has been my favorite subject in school since the second

grade. The encouragement and support of great art teachers who acknowledged and

expanded my talent bought forth a hard working and determined attitude. When I started

studying graphic design two years ago, I quickly learned that my traditional art skills

transitioned smoothly into media design on computers. The same great textures and color

combinations can be generated on a computer to create feelings that provoke interest

in people


I attended college to study graphic design, because I wanted to expand my experi-

ence in using digital art programs. Through my studies at the Art Institute of Indianapolis, I

have learned to use adobe CS4 and 5. I initially used CS4 in Illustrator, Photoshop, Flash,

In Design, Premier, Encoder, Dream Weaver, and After Effects. My portfolio is diverse, but

I have a special interest in sports, animals, and music. Designing DVD video menus is

one of my greatest passions. At the Art Institute of Indianapolis I have completed projects

designing ad campaigns, design briefs, and standards manuals. I enjoy and have strong

skills in designing and formatting web banners. I particularly enjoy animating my web

banners. I am a person who goes above and beyond what is ask of me if I think it will

make the final design better.


I have always had an interest graphic design and how it has evolved throughout the

past decades. I look forward to using the design skills I have learned at the Art Institute of

Indianapolis to help me to continue to inspire others with my work
